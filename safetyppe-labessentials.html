SAFETY PPE'S & LAB ESSENTIALS


pH, TDS (Total Dissolved Solids), and conductivity meters are essential tools in various industries, particularly in fields related to water quality assessment, agriculture, hydroponics, aquaculture, and environmental monitoring.

  • pH Meter: A pH meter measures the acidity or alkalinity of a solution, indicating its hydrogen ion concentration. It's crucial for maintaining optimal pH levels in water systems, such as pools, aquariums, and agricultural irrigation.
  • TDS Meter (Total Dissolved Solids): TDS meters measure the concentration of dissolved solids in a liquid, typically expressed in parts per million (ppm) or milligrams per liter (mg/L). Dissolved solids can include salts, minerals, metals, and other organic compounds.
  • Conductivity Meter: Conductivity meters measure the ability of a solution to conduct electrical current, which correlates with ion concentration. They are often used interchangeably with TDS meters since higher conductivity usually indicates higher TDS levels.


These meters come in various forms, from handheld portable devices to benchtop models with advanced features for precise measurements and data logging. Regular calibration and maintenance are essential to ensure accurate readings and reliable performance. Additionally, understanding the principles behind these meters is crucial for interpreting results and making informed decisions regarding water and soil management, environmental protection, and product quality control.
